Mechanical Hardware

Jan 2026 – Apr 2026
Safe-Slicer: Mechanical Automation
MENG 1105 - Yale University
Problem
People with limited hand mobility often struggle to safely slice vegetables. Figuring out how to automate this without using any electronics required some clever mechanical loops.
My Role
Engineered a 100% mechanical, one-handed slicer. I designed a "Cut and Advance" loop driven entirely by the motion of a single handle.
Execution
I integrated a one-way needle-roller bearing on a freewheel shaft to grip the food during the cut, then used a linear gear rack and timing belt to advance the PVC conveyor forward by exactly one slice-thickness on the upstroke.
Tools
Onshape, 3D-Printed PLA, machining metals, gears, belts, and more.
KinematicsMachiningGear TrainsConveyor Systems
Transmission Assembly

How I Think

I
Constraints Are the Brief

Cost, lack of electronics, one-hand operation — these are not limitations; they form the design DNA and drive true innovation.

II
User Defines the System

Real people, not abstractions. Empathy is the primary engineering metric when designing tools that interface with humanity.

III
Mechanics Before Electronics

Robust, repairable, accessible. Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication. A mechanical failure should always have a tangible fix.
